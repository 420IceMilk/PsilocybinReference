What is it?

What it is:

Psilocybin is an Schedule I ingestible hallucinogenic substance from psylocybin mushrooms. After consumption, the psilocybin is absorbed by the stomach and is converted into psilocyn. It activates serotonin receptors that are most commonly located in the prefrontal cortex and within 30 minutes, the hallucinogenic effects begin. These hallucinogenic effects usually last between 4 and 6 hours but more uncommonly can last for up to several days.

Why do it?

Many people do it after hearing anecdotal accounts from other people about increased emotional and spiritual insight. It has also been used in some religious practices for centuries because of its hallucinogenic effects. There have been studies that have correlated psilocybin use to positive depression outcomes. There is also ongoing research into potential benefits for other health conditions such as: anxiety disorders, cluster headaches, and ADHD.
